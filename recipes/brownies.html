Brownies

A rich, fudgy square of chocolate brownie with a shiny crinkle top

Nothing beats a homemade brownie that is dense, chewy and packed with deep chocolate flavour. This recipe skips the cake-like texture in favour of a rich, fudgy center that melts in your mouth. The key to achieving iconic, shiny crinkled top layer is vigorously whisking your sugar and eggs together before folding in the melted chocolate and butter.

This is a fantastic recipe for practicing your baking foundations because it requires careful measuring and gently mixing. It yields a decadent treat that tastes infinitely better than anything you can make from a boxed mix, making it a guaranteed crowd-pleaser for friends and family.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup unusweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F(175°) and grease an 8x8 inch baking pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk the melted butter and granulated sugar together until smooth.
  3. Sift in the cocoa powder, all-purpose flour, and salt.
  4. Gently fold the dry ingredients into the wet mixture using a spatula just until the flour disappears-do not overmix!
  5. Spread the batter evenly into your prepared baking pan.
  6. Bake for 20 to 22 minutes until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs attached.
  7. Enjoy your sweet fudgy brownies!
